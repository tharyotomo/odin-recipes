Gado-Gado

Description

A fresh and healthy Indonesian salad, that you can cook in your home this weekend. And surely, it will satisfy all of our vegetarian followers… and those who aren’t too!

Ingredients

  • 200g green beans (or asparagus beans), cut into 5cm pieces and blanched
  • 200g soybean sprouts, blanched
  • 200g white cabbage, sliced and blanched
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, cut into quarters
  • 4 small cucumbers, sliced
  • 300g firm tofu
  • 300g tempeh
  • 2 potatoes, boiled and diced (optional)
  • Lontong or rice cake (optional)
  • Krupuk or emping (crackers, optional)
  • 300g peanuts (no salt)
  • 5 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 2 red chillies, seeded and chopped
  • 60g palm sugar
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind extract
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Blanch all vegetables: briefly immerse them in boiling water, so that they don’t get fully cooked: let them boil for a few moments, bring them out with a foamer and pass them right away by cold water.
  2. Prepare the sauce by frying the peanuts in a hot pan with a tablespoon of oil. Stir frequently for a few minutes, until slightly brown.
  3. Mix peanuts, garlic and chilli with a mortar for a smooth paste.
  4. In a saucepan, combine the processed mixture with tamarind extract, lemon juice and water (about 50 ml). Continue simmering to achieve the desired consistency, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat.
  5. Cut the tofu into 2 cm cubes. Fry the tofu in a frying pan with hot oil to lightly brown on all sides.
  6. Cut the tempeh and fry for a few minutes.
  7. Place all ingredients in a dish and serve with the peanut sauce on top.
